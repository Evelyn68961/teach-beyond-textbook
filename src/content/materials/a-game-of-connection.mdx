## The story

The Chinese New Year holiday this year was quite unusual for me. This is mainly because I chose to take the night shift while my colleagues were enjoying time off with their family and friends. With only two days off before work began, I decided to visit my grandparents and aunt. I enjoyed two relaxing days at their home, and during that time, I stumbled upon something unexpected that sparked my interest.

It all started with a game of Chinese chess against my grandpa. He had been trying to teach me how to play ever since I was a child, but I had never truly felt motivated to learn it seriously. This time, however, was different. My original intention was simply to keep my grandpa company, but once the game began, I found myself genuinely enjoying it — especially when I managed to seize small advantages from his occasional slips. Chinese chess is undeniably complex, but it was precisely that complexity that gave rise to a deep sense of achievement and fun.

I'm not sure when I will next have the chance to sit across the chessboard from my grandpa again, but on my way back to Taipei, I downloaded a teaching app to continue learning on my own. Although I honestly don't have much free time to refine my skills, I hope to at least nurture it as a small hobby — and, more importantly, as a meaningful connection between my grandpa and me.
