## The story

After submitting my manuscript to my first target journal, I finally allowed myself a short breath of relief — but only a short one. As a research pharmacist, I knew the next task was already waiting: applying for an internal grant with an entirely new proposal. For me, every finished project is less of an ending and more of a stepping stone toward the next question worth asking.

True to my challenge-seeking nature, I did not want this new proposal to be a simple extension of what I had already done. Instead, I wanted to push the current workflow further — to refine it so that it could answer a research question more comprehensively, and, more importantly, address a clinical problem with greater meaning for patients. That ambition was exactly where I began drafting.

The aim was intriguing, but so was the challenge that came with it. The more I read and thought, the more I realized how much I still did not know. Where should I begin? Which methods would give me the most reliable answers? How could I build a proposal strong enough to convince the review committee? These questions circled in my mind day and night, and I often found myself staring at a blank document, unsure which sentence to write first.

Then, in the middle of this uncertainty, something unexpected happened. While browsing research articles one evening, I happened to come across a paper written by a former teacher of mine from school. For a moment, I could hardly believe it — the same teacher who had once introduced me to the world of research was now publishing in a field closely related to the very problem I was trying to solve.

I brought the discovery to my supervisor the next morning, and with his permission, I reached out to her right away. To my delight, she replied warmly and generously agreed to guide me on the path ahead. In that instant, the long road before me no longer felt quite so lonely. What had seemed like an overwhelming challenge suddenly became an exciting journey — one I would not have to walk alone.

Sometimes, I think, the universe has a quiet way of placing the right people in our lives exactly when we need them most.
