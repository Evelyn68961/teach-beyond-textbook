## The story

"You're making rapid progress! Keep up the good work — I believe you can make it!" The voice drifted toward me as I walked to school. A young woman with long brown hair was chatting cheerfully with a first-grade girl. She was Jenny, one of the English teachers at my school. The moment I noticed how lovely she looked in her elegant blouse, she flashed a warm smile at the little girl, and I suddenly realized they were rehearsing for the public English speaking competition. "Make it?" the girl asked with an innocent smile. "It means I'll get a great score and win the competition, right?"

In that instant, time froze, and memories came flooding back……

Five years ago, I decided to enter a speaking competition to challenge myself, and Jenny — then a novice teacher — was the one who guided me. First, she showed me how to move an audience with my voice. Perhaps because it was her very first time tutoring one-on-one, I could sense a hint of nervousness in her. Yet she always eased her tension with slow, steady breathing, and rehearsed with me over and over without ever sighing or complaining. Still, my enthusiasm began to fade. Endless practice made me wonder whether I had chosen the wrong path, and whether I should give up.

Shouldn't I have avoided this difficult road, lined with frustration and hurdles, in the first place?…… I felt as if I were drifting, lost, in a vast ocean — until she spoke one sentence that lifted my heart from helpless to hopeful: "I believe you can make it! You haven't given up so far, have you?"

Her words stayed with me, gently reminding me that my hesitation was holding me back. Yes — keep going!

"You're the best student I've ever taught."……

With my heart pounding, I stepped onto the stage on the day of the competition, wearing a grateful smile.

I had nearly given up on myself, and I had even blamed myself for the decision I made back then. But this ordinary teacher had given me positive energy and hope from beginning to end — offering unconditional support even when I had stopped believing in myself.

A soft breath pulled me from memory back to the present. "Keep going!" I cheered silently in my mind — for the past version of me, and for the one yet to come.
