## The story

Last weekend, while I was scrolling through photos on my iPad with a cup of hot milk tea, several pictures caught my eye. As soon as I realized they were taken during my graduation trip, countless memories flooded my mind and gentle smiles warmed my heart. The wonderful times from four months ago came alive once again. Although the photos were not taken very well, they still captured those precious moments perfectly.

On the first day of the trip, we visited an amusement park. After a few hours' drive, we finally arrived at Janfusun Fancyworld (劍湖山世界) — home to thrilling rides like the G5 and the Skyscraper. The scenes at the amusement park were indescribable, flashing before my eyes one after another. I truly enjoyed the fantastic atmosphere with my friends, and even before we arrived at the hotel, I was already soaked in happiness. The next day, we had a guided tour of a museum. Though it was a bit dull for me, who wouldn't want to spend time with their best friends?

This was the last field trip of elementary school — the last chance to let loose and create lasting memories with friends. "The friendships, memories, and knowledge you have built in this school — all of you have completed thousands of lessons and overcome countless challenges. In fact, you are much better than you think."

The principal's speech brought this three-day, two-night trip to a close. The excitement of counting down the final three months had hidden six years of growth beneath it. I took away three reminders from this wonderful trip: first, cherish every moment that brings you joy; second, life may seem long on the surface, but it is actually very short; and last, you are the best version of yourself — be confident and look back at how far your journey has brought you.
