## The story

On a quiet Sunday morning, golden sunlight filtered through the curtains and filled my room with warmth. "What a peaceful moment," I murmured, stretching lazily — but today, there was no smile on my face. The massive project assigned by Ms. Jacky had swallowed my entire weekend whole.

Ms. Jacky was, without question, the strictest English teacher in our school. She had a reputation for handing out assignments so difficult that even she herself sometimes struggled with the answers. She only assigned homework once every three months, but each time felt like marching straight into battle — and we had to survive it four times a year.

I dragged myself to my desk and reached for my English dictionary, determined to tackle the questions one by one. Just then, a piece of paper fluttered down from the shelf above me. I picked it up — it was an exquisite envelope, decorated with tiny flowers. The sender was Sandy, a friend from school.

Sandy was unlike anyone else I knew. In an age of instant messages and social media, she still preferred to communicate through handwritten letters. Filled with curiosity, I gently peeled the tape off the envelope and carefully unfolded the letter inside:

> "The homework Ms. Jacky assigned this week is the hardest thing I have ever encountered! I want to talk to her tomorrow and explain the obstacles we are facing and why finishing this assignment feels impossible… But the truth is, I don't have the courage to confront the strictest teacher in school by myself. Will you face this challenge with me?"

I read the last line of her small, neat handwriting, and a strange wave of emotion washed over me — a tangled mixture of guilt and admiration. Guilt, because I realized I was just as timid as Sandy. I, too, had once thought about raising these concerns with Ms. Jacky, but each time, fear held me back. And admiration, because Sandy had found the courage to at least reach out and ask for help. I set the letter down and sat in silence for what felt like an eternity, turning her words over in my mind.

Eventually, we made our decision. Hearts pounding, we walked up to Ms. Jacky together after class. To our surprise, she did not scold us. Instead, her expression softened.

"I never intended to become a burden to you," she said quietly, her eyes wide. "All I want is to lay a strong foundation for your future. I am sorry — I should have been more aware of how you were feeling." She reached out and gently patted our heads. "Thank you for telling me this." Then she paused, and her final words caught me completely off guard: "You are the bravest students I have ever seen."
