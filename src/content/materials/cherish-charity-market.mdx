## The story

Let us pause for a moment and reflect on something we often overlook — the simple act of being content with what we have.

Take a deep breath, quiet your mind, and consider your own life. Chances are, your life is stable, safe, and filled with comfort. Yet many of us would protest: "My life is nothing special — just endless work, with countless obstacles creeping up on me day and night," or "Exams, frustration, exhaustion — my dream is to have unlimited wealth, live in a golden castle, and never worry again."

Everyone deserves to be well-fed and well-clothed. However, the harsh reality is that the enormous gap between the rich and the poor leads to vastly different standards of living. How do we even begin to address such a deeply rooted problem? Have you ever pondered this question with care? If not, allow me to share my own small but meaningful experience.

Every year on Children's Day, my school organized a charity market where each class had to sell something. Students could choose whether to donate the earnings or keep them. My group decided to make handmade snowflake Q cakes together. Rather than playing it safe and selling popular items like ice cream or fried instant noodles — which would have guaranteed a steady income — we ventured down an uncertain path, determined to stand out from the crowd.

Fortunately, perseverance paid off. The effort we poured into our project was rewarded with an overwhelming wave of compliments and support from customers. In the end, we donated every penny we earned to charity.

A few days later, I discovered an exquisite letter tucked beneath my desk mat. It was filled with heartfelt words of gratitude and blessing. To this day, I am still unsure whether the letter was truly written by someone from the charity or simply arranged by our teachers as encouragement — but either way, it remains the most touching feedback I have ever received.

"Small gestures can offer the greatest blessing." That final line from the letter is something I will carry with me forever.
