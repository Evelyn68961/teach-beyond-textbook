## The story

When I decided to become a pharmacist for my first job, I also made a bold decision to learn coding at the same time. It was not because medicine no longer gave me passion and motivation, but because I wanted a career that was not limited by time and space — and I gradually realized that focusing solely on medicine and pharmacy would not lead me there. Before embarking on this journey, the goal was simple: work hard at the pharmacy, study hard on coding after work. But as I started down the path, I found that this seemingly simple plan was actually mission impossible. Being nearly a complete beginner, my hours after work were already filled with clinical studies, which drastically cut into my time for coding. On top of that, as AI grew increasingly powerful, I began to doubt my initial decision. Was the money worth it? Was the time worth it? A function that took me hours or even days to build from scratch could be done by AI in less than ten minutes. The difficulties I encountered and the realization of AI's capabilities left me feeling truly depressed for a while — until something began to shift at work.

As my skills accumulated and I achieved strong performance in the workplace, I was assigned the role of research pharmacist instead of purely dispensing medication. This shift in position gave me more space and time to explore new skills and possibilities. One project I am working on is a nearly mature educational system that teaches pharmacists how to conduct analysis in clinical studies. Beyond that, I am also trying to build a system that converts slides and PDF files into short podcasts, allowing pharmacists to listen during their free time. These are real challenges, and I do feel nervous about completing them. Yet despite the anxiety and fear, I find that I am able to keep moving forward. I am not sure where these experiments will ultimately lead, but the eight hours I spend at work now feel more meaningful and fulfilling. They give me a way to contribute to my workplace by building genuinely useful tools that no other engineer could create. Becoming a true software engineer may not be the path I ultimately take, but I am glad I went down this road and built a whole new identity and role that I never could have imagined before.
